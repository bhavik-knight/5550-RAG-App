Question: What is Crosswalk guards?
Answer: Crosswalk guards are individuals who direct the movement of children along or across highways going to or from school. They signal drivers to stop by holding up a stop sign (paddle) facing the vehicle, and drivers must obey them (p. 43).

Question: What to do if moving through an intersection with a green signal?
Answer: According to the context, when moving through an intersection with a green signal, you should proceed with caution, but also follow some guidelines. Specifically, it is stated that "through traffic is controlled by the green signal lights" (page 44) when facing a green signal light and a green arrow. Additionally, it is advised to "Make a proper lane change before entering the intersection. Never change lanes while you are moving through an intersection" (page 44). Also, "yield the right of way to oncoming vehicles and any pedestrians that may be in your path" (page 39).
Sources: DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 3), DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 7), DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 8), DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 4)

Question: What to do when approached by an emergency vehicle?
Answer: When approached by an emergency vehicle, you should:
• Move to the right edge or curb of the road and stop completely.
• Remain stopped until the emergency vehicle has passed, or until you are directed to move by a peace officer (page 45, "Emergency Vehicles").
Sources: DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 31), DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 29), DH-Chapter2.pdf (Page 9)
